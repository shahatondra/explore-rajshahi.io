About Rajshahi

Rajshahi is one of the major cities in northwestern Bangladesh, situated on the northern bank of the Padma River. Known as the "Silk City" and the "Education City of Bangladesh," Rajshahi is famous for its rich cultural heritage, historical landmarks, delicious mangoes, and traditional silk industry.


The region is home to many remarkable historical sites, including Puthia Rajbari, Bagha Mosque, Natore Rajbari, Kusumba Mosque, Varendra Research Museum, and several ancient temples and palaces. These attractions reflect the architectural beauty and historical significance of Bengal's past.


Besides its historical treasures, Rajshahi offers beautiful river views, peaceful parks, museums, educational institutions, and vibrant local markets, making it one of the most attractive tourist destinations in Bangladesh.
