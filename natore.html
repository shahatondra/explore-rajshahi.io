About Natore Rajbari

Natore Rajbari, also known as Natore Palace or Pagla Raja’s Palace, is one of the most important historical landmarks in Bangladesh. Located in Natore, it was the royal residence of the Rajshahi Raj family, a powerful zamindar dynasty of Bengal.


The palace was established by Raja Ramjivan Moitra, who founded the Natore Raj family in the early 18th century. It became the administrative and residential center of the Rajshahi Zamindari, one of the largest zamindari estates in Bengal.

Natore Rajbari is closely associated with Rani Bhabani, a renowned philanthropist and zamindar. After the death of her husband, Raja Ramkanta Moitra, she efficiently managed and expanded the estate. Rani Bhabani became famous for her charitable works, including the construction of temples, roads, ponds, and other public welfare projects.
