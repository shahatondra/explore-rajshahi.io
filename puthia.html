About Puthia Rajbari

Puthia Rajbari or Panchani Zamindarbari is the residence of Maharani Hemantkumari Devi. Puthia Rajbari in Rajshahi is one of the archaeological heritages of Bengal. In 1895, Maharani Hemantkumari Devi built the present two-storey rectangular palace in the attractive Indo-European architectural style.


During the Mughal period in the 17th century, the Puthia zamindari was the oldest of the various states of Bengal. It is said that after a certain Nilambar received the title of 'Raja' from the Mughal Emperor Jahangir, it came to be known as the Puthia Rajbari. In 1744, the zamindari was divided. In that division, the eldest son of the zamindari received five and a half annas of the property and the other three sons received three and a half annas. The zamindari system existed until 1950. When the system was abolished, the zamindari of the Puthia Rajbari also became extinct. But even though the zamindari system was abolished, their palaces, temples and other structures built during that period still survive. This palace was built in 1895 by Maharani Hemanta Kumari Devi in honour of her mother-in-law Maharani Sarat Sundari Devi.
