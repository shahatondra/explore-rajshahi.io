About Bagha Mosque

The Bagha Mosque (Bengali: বাঘা মসজিদ) is a mosque located at Bagha, 40 kilometres (25 mi) southeast of Rajshahi in the Rajshahi Division of Bangladesh.


The mosque was built in 930 AH (1523/1524 CE) by Sultan Nusrat Shah, son of Alauddin Shah, the founder of the Husain Shahidynasty. Later, the mosque was renovated at different times and when the domes of the mosque collapsed, the destroyed mosque was rebuilt in 1897.

Initially, its roof was over ten domes but later those disintegrated. The mosque was constructed with brick and stone plinth. An earthquake in 1897 destroyed all the domes.
