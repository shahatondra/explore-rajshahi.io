Rajshahi College

Educational Institute and a Historical Landmark

About Rajshahi College

Rajshahi College is one of the oldest and most prestigious institutions of higher education in Bangladesh. Located in Rajshahi, the college has played a major role in spreading modern education in the northern region of the country.


Rajshahi College was established in 1873 with the financial assistance of Raja Haralal Roy Bahadur of Dubalhati. Land for the college was donated by Raja Haranath Roy. The institution grew out of the earlier Bowalia English School, founded in 1828, which later became Rajshahi Collegiate School. In 1878, the college was granted first-grade status, allowing it to offer degree courses under the University of Calcutta. This made Rajshahi College an important center for higher education in Bengal during the British colonial period.
