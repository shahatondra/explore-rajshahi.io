Dighapatia Rajbari

Dighapatia Rajbari, now officially known as Uttara Ganabhaban , is one of the most famous historical palaces in Bangladesh. Located in Natore, it was originally the royal residence of the Dighapatia Raj family and later became an important northern government residence for the state.


Dighapatia Rajbari was established in 1734 by Raja Doyaram Roy, who served as the Dewan (minister) of Rani Bhabani of Natore. Rani Bhabani, impressed by his loyalty and administrative skills, granted him the Dighapatia estate.

The Dighapatia Raj family became one of the most influential zamindar families in Bengal. Over time, the palace complex expanded and was developed into a grand royal residence. After the devastating 1897 earthquake, the palace was extensively rebuilt and beautifully renovated under the supervision of Raja Pramada Nath Roy.
